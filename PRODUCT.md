# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Internal ADB analysts and researchers. Professionals working with development data across the Asia-Pacific region — they expect dense, accurate information presented clearly. They use this platform to track digital transformation metrics, explore geographic data, and report findings. They value precision and reliability over novelty.

## Product Purpose

digiASIA Insights is an integrated platform leveraging data, visuals, and maps to track digital transformation across ADB's developing member countries. Success means an analyst can navigate to a relevant map, indicator, or index benchmark in seconds, and trust the data they see without second-guessing the interface.

## Brand Personality

Precise, trusted, modern. Authoritative without being cold. The interface should feel like a serious institutional tool — a peer-reviewed reference, not a SaaS product. Three words: **measured, credible, clear**.

**Emotional goals:** Confidence and clarity. Users should feel they are working with a well-organized, high-integrity system. No visual noise, no ambiguity.

## Anti-references

- Startup/consumer aesthetics (Airbnb-style warmth, Notion-style soft pastels)
- Dark dashboards with neon or glowing accents
- Playful illustrations or decorative iconography
- SaaS landing-page clichés: gradient hero stats, floating metric cards, hero-metric templates
- Glassmorphism, blur-based depth, rainbow gradients as decoration
- Anything that undermines institutional credibility or reads as "tech company marketing"

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — every visual element must serve communication. Remove anything that doesn't help the user understand data faster.
2. **Institutional authority** — the interface should feel peer-reviewed. Clean geometry, precise spacing, no visual tricks.
3. **Data legibility first** — typography hierarchy must make numbers and labels effortlessly scannable. Lining numbers for all statistics.
4. **Whitespace is structure** — generous margins and padding separate content zones. The grid is the design, not a support system for decoration.
5. **Color carries meaning** — ADB Blue anchors the primary identity. Accent colors signal state or category and must never be used decoratively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Light mode only. WCAG AA minimum. Designed for professional, desktop-first use with responsive support down to mobile. No reduced-motion considerations assumed, but transitions should be brief (≤200ms) and state-driven, never choreographic.
